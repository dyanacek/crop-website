SOME HUNGER FACTS

According to the latest United Nations report on hunger, one of every six people in this world, one sixth of all humanity, suffers from chronic hunger. That is an increase of 100 million people from this same time last year, bringing the total number of people suffering from serious chronic hunger to just over 1 billion people worldwide. In our own community of Seattle, hunger is rising. Recent reports from local Food Banks all show an unprecedented growth in the need for their services.

When we focus on mortality rates, hunger is never specifically listed. But it is all too frequently a major contributor to death.

According to Jean Ziegler (the United Nations Special Rapporteur on the Right to Food for 2000 to March 2008), mortality due to malnutrition accounted for 58% of the total mortality in 2006: "In the world, approximately 62 millions people, all causes of death combined, die each year. In 2006, more than 36 millions died of hunger or diseases due to deficiencies in micronutrients"

Based on these experts, more people die prematurely from hunger than from all other causes combined. Yet unlike cancer or AIDS, a simple, widely available cure is actually known.

Worldwide:

  • 925 million people do not have enough to eat - that’s nearly three times the population of the U.S.
  • 98 percent of the world’s hungry live in developing countries.
  • Malnutrition leads to nearly one third of all childhood deaths before age 5.
  • More than 60 percent of the world’s hungry are women.
  • Hunger kills more people annually than AIDS, malaria and tuberculosis combined.
  • One in seven people will go to bed hungry tonight.
  • One in four children in developing countries is underweight.

In the United States:

  • 50.2 million Americans live in food-insecure households.
  • 17.2 million children in our country don’t get enough healthy food to eat.
  • Households with children experience almost double the food insecurity rate of childless households.
  • 5.6 million households relied on a food pantry one or more times in 2009.
  • 1 in 8 Americans currently participate in SNAP (formerly the Food Stamp program)
